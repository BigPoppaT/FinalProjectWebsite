Lizzie Velasquez TedTalk

Summary

Our teacher assigned us a project about Lizzie Velasquez. She has this rare syndrome that prevents her from gaining weight. In the video she showed us, Lizzie talked about the challenges she faced while growing up and the daily struggles she continues to endure. She also shared some valuable insights on how to find purpose in life, regardless of your appearance or the obstacles you encounter. It was a pretty eye-opening experience, to be honest.

Rhetorical Devices

Irony - Lizzie Velasquez uses irony when she mentions that some people wonder how there could be benefits to her condition, like only being able to see out of one eye. This ironic statement challenges stereotypes and expectations, prompting the audience to reconsider their assumptions.

Hyperbole - Lizzie Velasquez exaggerates her roommate's observation about her late-night snacking by saying, "I could hear you at 12:30 reaching under your bed to get food." This exaggeration adds humor and emphasizes her point about eating habits.

Metaphor - When describing her backpack, which looked like a "turtle shell," Lizzie Velasquez uses a metaphor to vividly convey its size and her small stature. This helps the audience visualize her childhood experiences.
